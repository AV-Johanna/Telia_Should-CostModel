Antenna Should-Cost
Model

A procurement intelligence tool for understanding the true cost structure of passive and active antennas across Telia's operating markets. Built to support supplier negotiations and sourcing decisions.

Antenna types covered
Passive single-band
2-port and 4-port. Used for legacy 2G/3G coverage and rural deployments.
Passive multi-band (RET)
8-port and 12-port with Remote Electrical Tilt. Dominant in Telia's current network.
Active Massive MIMO
32T32R and 64T64R. Separate cost logic — integrated radios, power amplifiers, beamforming chips. 5G urban densification.

Key assumptions
  • ~85% of global antenna production is in China. Model defaults to Chinese manufacturing with switchable origin.
  • Labour cost based on country manufacturing wage index (ILO/national statistics, 2023).
  • Raw material prices from LME, Shanghai Metal Market, ICIS — 2023 annual averages.
  • Corporate OH (SG&A, R&D) derived from 11 supplier annual reports, expressed as % of revenue.
  • Logistics = sea freight + inland haulage + customs duty. Per-unit estimates based on antenna weight class.
  • Passive antennas: standard BoM framework. Active MIMO: separate cost logic for electronics-heavy components.
  • All outputs in SEK. USD used as calculation base with Riksbank 2023 average exchange rate (10.61 SEK/USD).
What this model does NOT cover
  • Installation and site integration costs (civil works, mounting, cabling)
  • Software licensing for active/MIMO antennas (beamforming algorithms, network management)
  • Warranty, maintenance or service contract costs post-delivery
  • Telia's internal cost of procurement operations or project management
  • Radio Unit (RU/RRU) costs — antennas only, not the full RAN stack
  • Custom or non-standard antenna designs (model covers standard commercial variants)
  • Supplier profit margin / recommended selling price — cost build-up only
  • Currency hedging costs or FX volatility adjustments

Supplier Benchmarks
Corporate financials from 11 antenna manufacturers — used to calibrate overhead rates
How corporate overhead is calculated
Methodology: SG&A, R&D and Other Corporate OH are sourced from each supplier's annual report as a percentage of total revenue. These percentages are then converted to a cost-per-antenna by applying them to the direct material cost. This mirrors how suppliers structure their internal costing — overhead scales roughly with material throughput. The model lets you choose which supplier or peer group to use as your benchmark.
